Tennis Lineup

Smart lineup management for USTA tennis team captains.

Tennis Lineup is a web app that helps USTA team captains manage their roster and generate optimal match lineups. It's focused on captains — individual players don't need an account.

It works across all competitive levels, from 2.5 mixed doubles to 4.5 men's leagues.

What you can do

  • Build a roster — Add players manually or auto-fill from TennisRecord profiles
  • Track ratings & stats — Dynamic ratings, court preferences, season win/loss records
  • Manage partnerships — Record doubles pairings and their performance trends
  • Generate lineups — Get optimal assignments across all five positions instantly

How it works

1

Build your roster

Add players with their dynamic ratings, court preferences, and line designations.

2

Mark availability

Before each match, check off who can play. You need at least 8 for a full lineup.

3

Scout the opponent

Enter their roster — or skip to Quick Mode if you don't have it.

4

Generate

Click Generate Optimal Lineup. Review the assignments and adjust if needed.

ℹ️
No installation needed. Tennis Lineup runs entirely in your browser and stores your roster data locally.

Quick Start

Get your roster set up and generate your first lineup.

Before you begin

Have your players' TennisRecord profile URLs handy — it makes adding players much faster. You'll need at least 8 players marked available to generate a complete lineup.

Step-by-step

1

Open the app

Navigate to tennislineup.ai in your browser. No login required.

2

Add your players

Click Add Player and enter each player's details. If they have a TennisRecord profile, paste the URL and click Lookup to auto-fill their name and rating.

3

Mark availability

Before your match, check off who can play. Use Select All or Clear All to manage quickly. You need at least 8 available for a full lineup.

4

Enter opponent info (optional)

Go to Opponent Scouting and add their players. Or choose Quick Mode to skip this step.

5

Generate your lineup

Click Generate Optimal Lineup. The app assigns players to S1, S2, D1, D2, and D3 based on ratings, preferences, and partnership history.

6

Review and finalize

Check the assignments and make any manual adjustments before submitting to your league.

💡
Pro tip: Add your doubles partnerships after building your roster. The generator uses partnership records to make smarter doubles assignments.

Partnerships

Track doubles partnerships and their performance trends.

A partnership represents a recorded pairing between two players. The generator uses this history to make smarter doubles assignments — prioritizing pairs that are trending up and separating pairs that are trending down.

Recording a partnership

1

Open the panel

Navigate to your roster and click Add / View Partnerships.

2

Choose players

Pick two different players from the dropdown menus. Order doesn't matter.

3

Enter match history

Add the number of wins and losses the pair has accumulated together.

4

Set the trend

Choose a trend indicator that reflects their current form.

5

Add notes (optional)

Include context about chemistry, communication, or scheduling preferences.

6

Save

Click Save Partnership to apply it to lineup generation.

Form fields

FieldRequiredNotes
Player A & BRequiredMust be two different players from your roster
Wins TogetherRequiredTotal wins as a pair
Losses TogetherRequiredTotal losses as a pair
TrendRequiredTrending Up, Stable, or Trending Down
NotesOptionalChemistry, communication, scheduling notes

How trends affect lineup generation

  • Trending Up — Pair receives priority placement in stronger doubles positions
  • Stable — Pair is placed based on combined ratings
  • Trending Down — Pair may be separated or moved to a lower slot
💡
Unrecorded pairs default to individual player ratings. The more partnership data you enter, the more accurate your generated lineups will be.

Player Ratings

Understand dynamic ratings and player status badges.

Dynamic ratings

The app uses USTA dynamic ratings — continuously updated numerical values that reflect a player's recent match performance. A rating of 3.91 indicates a strong 4.0-level player performing near the top of that level.

Ratings are sourced either through TennisRecord profile lookups (recommended) or manual entry. They're the primary factor the generator uses to rank players and assign positions.

Status badges

Each player card displays a badge reflecting their current performance trend:


🔥 Hot 📈 Improving ⚠️ Caution ★ CPT

BadgeMeaningEffect on lineup
HotWinning streak or above-rating performanceMay trigger position upgrade
ImprovingUpward rating momentum this seasonMay trigger position upgrade
CautionRecent losses or declining trendIncluded but flagged for manual review
CPTTeam captainNo effect on placement

Win/loss records

Player records display as W-L (%) format showing seasonal match results alongside the dynamic rating. Higher-rated players with stronger records receive top slots in the generated lineup.

ℹ️
Keep dynamic ratings current throughout the season. A stale rating from the prior year will skew lineup recommendations.

Generating a Lineup

Generate an optimal lineup from your available players.

⚠️
Minimum requirement: A complete lineup requires 5 positions — 2 singles and 3 doubles pairs (6 players). You need at least 8 available players to fill all positions.

Generation workflow

1

Mark available players

Go to your roster and check off who can play. Use Select All or Clear to manage quickly.

2

Enter opponent information

Go to Opponent Scouting and add their players, or choose Quick Mode to skip this step and optimize based on your roster alone.

3

Generate

Click Generate Optimal Lineup. The algorithm evaluates all available players and assigns them to positions.

4

Review and adjust

Check the S1, S2, D1, D2, D3 assignments. Make manual adjustments before finalizing.

What the algorithm considers

  • Current dynamic ratings
  • Court-side preferences (Ad, Deuce, or Flexible)
  • Line designations (Singles, Doubles, or Both)
  • Individual win percentages and seasonal records
  • Recorded doubles partnerships and performance trends

Quick Mode

When you don't have opponent data or want a fast answer, Quick Mode skips opponent analysis and optimizes purely from your roster. It still produces a valid, complete lineup.

Line Positions

Understand what each lineup position means.

A USTA team match consists of five positions: two singles and three doubles pairs. The generator assigns players to each based on ratings, preferences, and matchup data.

Each doubles pair includes an Ad side and Deuce side player. Setting accurate preferences helps the generator create stronger pairings.

Opponent Scouting

Enter your opponent's lineup before generating your own.

Scouting your opponent lets the generator factor in matchups when assigning positions — putting your strengths against their weaknesses where possible.

Entry modes

✓

Full Scouting

Enter each opponent player by name and optionally a TennisRecord URL. The system retrieves their rating automatically. Best when you have access to their roster from a league website or prior match.

→

Quick Mode

Skip opponent entry entirely. The generator optimizes based solely on your roster's strengths, preferences, and historical performance. Use this when speed matters or opponent data isn't available.

Positions to fill

  • S1 and S2 — singles players
  • D1, D2, D3 — three doubles pairs (six players total)

Roster paste

Use the paste function to populate opponent fields quickly. The tool parses flexible formats including names with ratings, position labels (S1, S2, D1…), or TennisRecord URLs. Review the parsed results before generating.

💡
TennisRecord URLs load opponent ratings automatically — no manual entry needed. Just paste the URL and the rating fills in.

FAQ

Answers to common questions about Tennis Lineup.


How do I add a player quickly? ▼
Paste a TennisRecord profile URL and click Lookup. It automatically fills in the player's name and dynamic rating — no manual entry needed.
What is a dynamic rating? ▼
A USTA dynamic rating is a continuously updated numerical value (e.g. 3.91) that reflects a player's recent match performance. Unlike year-end ratings, dynamic ratings update throughout the season.
What do the status badges mean? ▼
🔥 Hot — winning streak or above-rating performance

📈 Improving — upward rating trend this season

⚠️ Caution — recent losses or declining performance

★ CPT — team captain designation
Can I generate a lineup without opponent data? ▼
Yes. Choose Quick Mode (No Opponent Info) to generate a valid lineup optimized for your roster's strengths without any opponent information.
What if I have fewer than 8 available players? ▼
With fewer than 8 available players, some doubles positions may remain unfilled. The generator optimizes whatever positions it can fill with the players available.
How do partnerships affect the lineup? ▼
Trending Up partnerships receive priority in higher doubles slots. Trending Down pairs may be separated or moved to a lower position. Unrecorded pairs are placed based on individual ratings alone.
How do I edit a player after adding them? ▼
Click the info icon next to a player's name in the roster to open their detail view and edit any field.
What does "Flexible" court side mean? ▼
A Flexible player can play either Ad or Deuce side in doubles. The generator decides their placement based on what creates the strongest overall pair.

Troubleshooting

Fix common issues with roster management and lineup generation.

⚠️
If you encounter a persistent issue not listed here, try refreshing the page. Your roster data is preserved in browser local storage and will not be lost on refresh.

TennisRecord lookup returns no data
  • Use the full URL to the player's TennisRecord profile page
  • The player must have a public TennisRecord profile
  • If it fails, enter their name and dynamic rating manually
Lineup generator doesn't fill all positions
  • You need at least 8 available players for a complete lineup
  • Check that Singles-only players aren't blocking doubles slots
  • An incomplete roster will naturally leave some positions unfilled
Player is absent from the lineup despite being available
  • Check the player's line preference — a Singles-only player won't fill doubles slots
  • A large rating differential vs. the opponent may affect placement
  • Confirm the player's availability checkbox is checked
Opponent ratings aren't loading automatically
  • Verify the TennisRecord URL is valid and leads to a player profile page
  • Private or non-existent profiles won't return data — enter ratings manually
Partnership can't be saved
  • Both players must exist in your current roster
  • Make sure you clicked Save Partnership after filling out the form
