Tennis Lineup Optimizer

A smart lineup tool for USTA tennis captains. Enter your roster, mark who's available, add opponent ratings, and get the optimal lineup in seconds — built around real match data, partnership history, and court-side fit.

ⓘ
Works on any phone or desktop — no install needed. All data is stored locally in your browser.

What it does

The optimizer evaluates every valid player combination and picks the lineup most likely to win the most lines. It accounts for:

  • USTA dynamic ratings — the official estimated rating for each player
  • Win/loss records — current and prior season performance
  • Partnership history — how doubles pairs have actually performed together
  • Court side preference — Ad vs. Deuce side fit
  • Opponent scouting — optional ratings for each opponent player
  • Singles capability — only players you flag as singles-capable are placed in singles lines

Format

Designed for the standard USTA adult format: 2 singles lines (S1, S2) and 3 doubles lines (D1, D2, D3). Eight players are placed total.

🎯 How to Use the App

1
Configure your team
Tap the gear icon in the top right to set your team name, level (e.g. 4.0 Women's), and season year. This personalizes the header and record labels.
2
Build your roster
Tap + Add Player to add each player. Paste a TennisRecord URL to auto-fill their name and rating. All players are saved in your browser and persist between sessions.
3
Mark who's available
All players are selected by default. Tap any player to toggle them unavailable. Only available players are considered in the lineup.
4
Enter the opponent lineup (optional)
Use Full Scouting to enter opponent names and ratings, or Quick Mode to skip. You can paste a full roster at once using Paste Opponent Roster.
5
Generate and save
Tap Generate Optimal Lineup. Review the results, check alternatives, then tap Save / Print Lineup to export as a PDF or screenshot.
✨
Tip: You need at least 8 available players and at least 2 marked as singles-capable to generate a lineup.

👥 Building Your Roster

The roster starts empty — add your players once and they're saved permanently in your browser.

Adding a player

Tap + Add Player. The fastest way is to paste a TennisRecord.com profile URL — the app fetches the player's name and dynamic rating automatically. Then fill in:

FieldWhat to enter
Dynamic RatingAuto-filled from TennisRecord, or enter manually (e.g. 3.72).
Preferred SideAd (left court), Deuce (right court), or Flexible. Used for side-fit scoring.
Primary LineDoubles, Singles, or Both. Only players set to Singles or Both can be placed at S1/S2.
Win % estimateYour best guess at their win rate at this level (0–100). Used as a tiebreaker in scoring.
Current / Prior Season W-LMatch record for the current and previous year. Shown on the player card and factored into scoring.

Removing a player

Tap the × button in the top-right corner of any player card and confirm. The player is removed from the roster and any partnerships involving them are also removed.

Player profile

Tap a player's name (or the ⓘ icon) to open a profile sheet showing their season records, partnership history, and player patterns.

⚠
Player data is saved to your browser's local storage. It persists on the same device but won't sync to teammates' phones automatically.

🤝 Partnerships

Partnership data is one of the most powerful inputs to the optimizer. A pair with a strong track record gets a significant scoring bonus; pairs flagged as Avoid are heavily penalized.

Adding a partnership

Tap Partnerships, select two players, enter their combined wins and losses, choose a trend, and add optional notes. If a partnership already exists for that pair, saving replaces it.

TrendMeaningScore effect
Trending Up ▲Recent results are better than their historical average+12 pts

Flagging an Avoid pair

Check Flag as Avoid when saving a partnership to mark two players as incompatible. The optimizer will heavily penalize this pairing (−120 pts) and a red warning will appear if they end up in the same doubles line.

✨
Captain tip: Update partnership records after each match weekend so the optimizer learns from your team's actual results over the season.

🔍 Opponent Lineup

Full Scouting vs. Quick Mode

Full Scouting lets you enter opponent names and ratings per line. The optimizer matches your players against specific opponents to maximize rating edges. Quick Mode skips this and optimizes based on your team's internal strengths only.

Pasting a roster

Tap Paste Opponent Roster and paste in any format:

  • Plain list: Jane Smith 3.80 — sorted by rating and auto-assigned
  • Labeled: S1: Alice Brown 3.75 or D1: Carol Davis 3.68 / Eve Wilson 3.65
  • TennisRecord URLs — ratings fetched automatically

Linking opponent profiles

Tap the 🔗 icon next to any opponent slot to add a TennisRecord URL. Their name in the results will become a clickable link to their full profile.

⚡ Generating a Lineup

Once 8+ players are available the Generate Optimal Lineup button activates. The optimizer scores every valid combination and returns the top 5. Results appear below the button with the page scrolling automatically.

Minimum requirements

  • At least 8 available players
  • At least 2 players with Primary Line set to Singles or Both
⚠
If fewer than 2 singles-capable players are available, a message will prompt you to mark at least 2 players as singles-eligible before generating.

📈 Confidence Scores

Every line shows a confidence indicator. It's a calculated estimate based on rating gaps and win/loss history — not a guarantee.

Favorable (65%+)
Competitive (45–64%)
Tough (<45%)

Singles confidence formula

50 + (rating edge × 80) + (win% × 30), clamped to 20–90%.

Doubles confidence formula

50 + (rating edge × 60) + (partnership win% × 25), with a −30 penalty if the pair has an Avoid flag.

⚠
Confidence is most meaningful in Full Scouting mode with opponent ratings entered. In Quick Mode all opponents default to 3.70, so confidence primarily reflects your team's internal strengths.

⚙ How the Optimizer Works

The optimizer uses a brute-force combinatorial search — it scores every valid lineup and returns the top 5. No machine learning, no black box. Every score is deterministic and explainable.

1
Identify singles candidates
Players with Primary Line set to Singles or Both are sorted by singles score. The top 4–6 are tried as S1/S2 combinations.
2
Build the doubles pool
Remaining available players are sorted by rating × (0.5 + win%). The top 12 form the doubles pool to keep computation fast.
3
Generate all pairings
All ways to form 3 pairs from 6 players are enumerated (15 pairings), then all 6 ways to assign those pairs to D1/D2/D3 are tried.
4
Score and rank
Each complete lineup is scored by summing the five line scores. The highest-scoring lineup is recommended; the next 4 are shown as alternatives.
ⓘ
The optimizer doesn't lock your best pair into D1. It tries every line assignment — sometimes your strongest pair scores better at D2 to exploit a favorable matchup there.

❓ FAQ & Tips

The Generate button is grayed out.
You need at least 8 players marked available. Check the count below the roster grid — it tells you exactly how many are selected.
No players show up as singles candidates.
At least 2 players must have Primary Line set to Singles or Both. Edit their profiles via the player card's ⓘ info button, or remove and re-add them with the correct line setting.
The optimizer puts a lower-rated pair at D1 — is that right?
Yes, sometimes. If the weakest opponent happens to be at D1, placing a mid-tier but proven pair there while saving your best for D2 can be the higher-scoring strategy overall.
My players disappeared after clearing my browser.
All data is stored in localStorage, which is cleared when you clear your browser history or use private/incognito mode. For a permanent roster, the captain should bookmark the app on a dedicated device or export the lineup before clearing.
Can multiple captains use this on different devices?
The app is single-device — there's no sync or cloud storage. The simplest workaround is to designate one device as the "captain's device" and always use that for the official lineup.
Does the app send any data anywhere?
No. Everything runs in your browser. The only external requests are optional TennisRecord URL lookups (to fetch ratings), which go through a public CORS proxy. No player data is stored on any server.
How do I update a player's rating mid-season?
Remove the player and re-add them with the updated rating and record. Their partnerships will need to be re-entered. For frequent updates, consider using the TennisRecord URL lookup when adding — it pulls the latest dynamic rating automatically.
